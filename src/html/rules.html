These are The Rules to this Twitter Game...

Its like orienteering...

Seriously, it takes minimal effort and it may help you get that 10,000 steps

To Start a Game

Tweet start game to @gonzo4111 with your exact location (lat, long) and the bot should respond right away!

You'll get a response in about 1 - 5 minutes. It will be a Tweet to your account that will contain a link to a map.

Like this....

d13dlmp0uwngbc.cloudfront.net/loc.html?long0=144.9621474&lat0=-37.8121507&dist0=534.72915564&16214623

If you look at the web address you'll see dist0=123.456. This is how far in meters you are from where you need to be.

So make an educated guess and get somewhere.

To find out if you're close

tweet: @gonzo4111 ping. Make sure you tweet the word ping and direct it at @ the bot!

You'll get another map with your new and previous locations.

d13dlmp0uwngbc.cloudfront.net/loc.html.....

You may have been way off....

Keep moving, keep looking and keep tweeting ping.

ping

ping

ping

You'll get a max history of your three last pings on any map. The green dot is where you are, yellow is before that and so on.

Had enough?

Tweet quit..... That's it, game over.

Otherwise keep looking and when you're within 15m you're done!

You'll get notified you've won and the game will be over.

Also, don't mind any random digits that are on the tail of any tweets, it's just to avoid the double tweet issue.

Questions?

  • What are the circles?
    • They are the distance between you and the point you're after. You're at the center of the circle and the point could be anywhere on its circumference.
  • So when I have two circles overlapping, there are two areas the point could be?
    • Yes.
  • I tweeted my exact location but I got directed back here?
    • Have a look at your twitter settings under privacy. The location differs slightly between iPhone and Android etc. But you want to enable gps for this game to play.
  • Do I win anything?
    • No. At this point in time it's a solo game to give me a bit of a play with a few features of mysql, java, and aws.
